Fundamentals In Electronics

A passive electrical component that resists the passage of electricity is called a resistor. They are present in practically all electronic circuits and electrical networks. Ohms (Î©) are used to measure resistance. The resistance that develops when a resistor with a one volt (V) drop across its terminals experiences a current of one ampere (A). This resistance is known as an ohm. The voltage across the terminal ends determines how much current flows through them. Ohm's law is a representation of this ratio: R = V I There are numerous uses for resistors. Limiting electric current, voltage division, heat production, matching and loading circuits, gain control, and time constant setting are a few instances. They have resistance levels spanning more than nine orders of magnitude that are commercially available.

The National Electrical Manufacturers Association is known by its acronym, NEMA. It is an American trade group that stands for producers of medical imaging systems and electrical equipment. NEMA offers a platform for the development of technical standards, public policy advocacy, and market research pertaining to the electrical sector. Electrical items such as wire devices, motors, lighting, transformers, medical imaging equipment, and more are covered by NEMA standards. The design, production, and usage of electrical devices are made safer, more efficient, and more interoperable thanks to these standards.

An inductor is a wire coil that, when current passes through it, stores energy in a magnetic field. By creating a voltage that is opposed to the change, it resists changes in current. Electronics employ inductors for voltage management, signal processing, energy storage, and filtering.

An electrical energy-storing component in electronics is called a capacitor. It is made up of two plates that are kept apart by a non-conductive substance. An electric field is created when a voltage is applied, storing charge. In electrical circuits, capacitors are used for energy storage, signal filtering, coupling and decoupling, and timing.

A switch in electronics is a component that either allows or interrupts the flow of electrical current in a circuit. It's like a gateway that controls whether electricity can pass through a circuit, enabling devices to be turned on or off.

In electronics, "changeover" refers to a switch or relay that alters the connection of a circuit, allowing you to switch between different inputs, outputs, or directions of current flow. It provides flexibility in routing signals or power within a circuit.

A pushbutton in electronics is a switch that is pressed to make or break a circuit connection. It's commonly used for user input or to control devices, such as turning on/off lights or triggering actions in electronic systems.

A male plug IEC is a type of electrical connector commonly used for power cords and cables. It has prongs or pins that fit into a corresponding female socket or receptacle. It's often used in electronics to connect devices to power sources or other equipment.

A male plug NEMA is a type of electrical connector standardized by the National Electrical Manufacturers Association (NEMA). It features prongs or pins designed to fit into a corresponding NEMA female receptacle, commonly used for connecting devices to power sources in various electronic applications.

A female plug IEC is a type of electrical connector with receptacles designed to accept male IEC plugs. It's commonly used for power cords and cables in electronic devices, providing a connection point for the male plug to deliver electrical power..

A female plug NEMA is an electrical connector with receptacles designed to accept male NEMA plugs. It's commonly used for power connections in electronic devices and equipment, providing a connection point for the male plug to deliver electrical power.

A fuse in electronics is a safety device designed to protect circuits from excessive current. It consists of a thin wire that melts when current surpasses a certain threshold, interrupting the circuit and preventing damage to components.

The electrical line in electronics refers to the power supply or source of electricity that energizes electronic devices or circuits.

The ground in electronics serves as a reference point for voltage levels and a pathway for excess electrical current to safely dissipate.

A diode in electronics is a semiconductor device that allows current to flow in one direction while blocking it in the opposite direction. It's commonly used for rectification, signal demodulation, and voltage regulation in electronic circuits.

A DIAC, or diode for alternating current, is a type of semiconductor device that conducts current when the voltage across it reaches a certain threshold in either direction. It's commonly used in triggering devices for thyristors and other semiconductor switches in AC circuits.

A thyristor is a semiconductor device that acts as a switch, controlling the flow of electrical current in a circuit. It can be turned on by a small voltage pulse and remains conductive until the current flowing through it drops below a certain threshold. Thyristors are commonly used in power control applications such as motor speed control, lighting dimming, and AC power regulation.

A TRIAC, or triode for alternating current, is a semiconductor device that conducts current in both directions, allowing control of AC power. It can switch on and off based on an external triggering signal, making it useful for applications such as dimming lights and controlling motor speed in AC circuits.

An integrated circuit (IC) is a miniature electronic circuit comprised of interconnected semiconductor devices, such as transistors, resistors, and capacitors, fabricated onto a single semiconductor substrate. It's commonly used to perform various functions, such as amplification, signal processing, and digital logic, in electronic devices.

An amplifier in electronics is a device that increases the amplitude or strength of a signal. It takes a weak input signal and produces a stronger output signal, typically with the same waveform but larger in magnitude. Amplifiers are used in a wide range of electronic devices, including audio systems, radio transmitters, and communication networks, to boost signals for better transmission or processing.

An electric generator is a device that converts mechanical energy into electrical energy. It operates on the principle of electromagnetic induction, where a rotating coil within a magnetic field produces an electric current. Generators are commonly used to generate electricity in power plants, turbines, and portable generators for various applications.

A battery is a device that stores chemical energy and converts it into electrical energy when needed. It consists of one or more electrochemical cells that contain electrolytes and electrodes, allowing for the flow of electric charge. Batteries are used to power a wide range of electronic devices, from small gadgets like smartphones to larger applications like electric vehicles and backup power systems.

An ammeter is a device used to measure the electric current in a circuit

A voltmeter is a device used to measure the voltage or electrical potential difference between two points in a circuit.

An ohmmeter is a device used to measure electrical resistance in a circuit.

A frequency meter is a device used to measure the frequency of an electrical signal, typically in Hertz (Hz)..

A wattmeter is a device used to measure electrical power, typically in watts, in a circuit.

An electric clock is a timekeeping device powered by electricity, typically using a quartz crystal oscillator to regulate the timekeeping mechanism.

An antenna is a device used to transmit or receive electromagnetic signals, such as radio waves, in wireless communication systems.

A speaker is a device that converts electrical signals into sound waves, producing audible sound for audio playback or communication purposes.

A microphone is a device that converts sound waves into electrical signals, allowing for the capture and recording of audio.

A light bulb is an electrical device that produces light by passing an electric current through a filament, causing it to heat up and emit light.

Direct current (DC) is an electrical current that flows in one direction only, maintaining a constant polarity over time.

Alternating current (AC) is an electrical current that periodically reverses direction, changing polarity over time.

Positive polarity in electronics refers to a voltage or current where the higher potential is considered positive relative to the lower potential.

Negative polarity in electronics refers to a voltage or current where the lower potential is considered negative relative to the higher potential.

A relay is an electrically operated switch that uses an electromagnet to mechanically control the opening or closing of contacts, allowing it to control the flow of electrical current in a circuit.
